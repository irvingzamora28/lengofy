# Lesson 7: Basic Questions in German

Welcome! In this lesson, you'll learn essential German question words and how to use them to ask simple, everyday questions. Asking questions is a key skill to start conversations and gather information.

Here are some common German question words:

| German | English    | Example Question                | Translation                  |
|--------|------------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Wer    | Who        | Wer bist du?                   | Who are you?                |
| Was    | What       | Was machst du?                 | What are you doing?          |
| Wo     | Where      | Wo wohnst du?                  | Where do you live?           |
| Wann   | When       | Wann kommst du?                | When are you coming?         |
| Warum  | Why        | Warum lernst du Deutsch?       | Why are you learning German?|
| Wie    | How        | Wie geht es dir?               | How are you?                 |

Throughout this lesson, you'll practice forming questions using these words to communicate more naturally in German. Let’s start exploring how to ask questions with confidence!

## Grammar: Forming Questions in German

In German, basic questions often start with question words such as **Wer** (Who), **Was** (What), **Wo** (Where), **Wann** (When), **Warum** (Why), and **Wie** (How). The word order in German questions is important to understand.

### Common German Question Words

| English  | German  | Example Question      | Translation               |
| -------- | ------- | --------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Who      | Wer     | Wer bist du?          | Who are you?              |
| What     | Was     | Was machst du?        | What are you doing?       |
| Where    | Wo      | Wo wohnst du?         | Where do you live?        |
| When     | Wann    | Wann kommst du?       | When are you coming?      |
| Why      | Warum   | Warum lernst du?      | Why are you learning?     |
| How      | Wie     | Wie geht es dir?      | How are you?              |

### Question Structure

The typical question structure using question words in German is:

- **Question word + verb + subject + other elements**

Examples:

- **Wo wohnst du?** (Where do you live?)
- **Was machst du?** (What are you doing?)
- **Warum lernst du Deutsch?** (Why are you learning German?)

Notice the verb comes directly after the question word, followed by the subject.

### Key Points to Remember

- The **verb always directly follows** the question word.
- The **subject follows the verb**, unlike English where the subject often comes first.
- German questions do **not require auxiliary verbs** like "do."

<TipBox>
To form yes/no questions (without question words), start with the verb followed by the subject. For example:
"**Kommst du morgen?**" means "Are you coming tomorrow?"
This inversion of verb and subject is key in German question formation.
</TipBox>

<TipBox>
Use question words at the beginning of your sentence to form clear questions.
- "Wer" = Who, used to ask about people.
- "Was" = What, used for things or information.
- "Wo" = Where, used for locations.
- "Wann" = When, used for time.
- "Warum" = Why, asks reason or cause.
- "Wie" = How, asks manner or method.
Combining these with verbs and simple phrases will help get the information you need while traveling.
</TipBox>
