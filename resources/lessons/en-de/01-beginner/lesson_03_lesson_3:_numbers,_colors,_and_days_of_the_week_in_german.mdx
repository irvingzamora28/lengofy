## Introduction
Welcome to the lesson on numbers, colors, and days of the week in German! This lesson introduces essential vocabulary to build your language skills. Let's dive in!

## Numbers (Zahlen)

Learning numbers is fundamental. Here are the numbers from one to ten:

*   Eins (1) - *Ich habe **eins** Buch.* (I have **one** book.)
*   Zwei (2) - *Wir haben **zwei** Katzen.* (We have **two** cats.)
*   Drei (3) - *Es gibt **drei** Äpfel.* (There are **three** apples.)
*   Vier (4) - *Sie hat **vier** Stifte.* (She has **four** pens.)
*   Fünf (5) - *Er isst **fünf** Kekse.* (He eats **five** cookies.)
*   Sechs (6) - *Das sind **sechs** Vögel.* (Those are **six** birds.)
*   Sieben (7) - *Wir sehen **sieben** Sterne.* (We see **seven** stars.)
*   Acht (8) - *Es kostet **acht** Euro.* (It costs **eight** euros.)
*   Neun (9) - *Ich habe **neun** Freunde.* (I have **nine** friends.)
*   Zehn (10) - *Sie zählt bis **zehn**.* (She counts to **ten**.)

## Colors (Farben)

Knowing colors helps you describe the world around you:

*   Rot (Red) - *Die Rose ist **rot**.* (The rose is **red**.)
*   Blau (Blue) - *Der Himmel ist **blau**.* (The sky is **blue**.)
*   Grün (Green) - *Das Gras ist **grün**.* (The grass is **green**.)
*   Gelb (Yellow) - *Die Sonne ist **gelb**.* (The sun is **yellow**.)
*   Schwarz (Black) - *Die Katze ist **schwarz**.* (The cat is **black**.)
*   Weiß (White) - *Der Schnee ist **weiß**.* (The snow is **white**.)
*   Orange (Orange) - *Die Orange ist **orange**.* (The orange is **orange**.)
*   Lila (Purple) - *Die Blume ist **lila**.* (The flower is **purple**.)

## Days of the Week (Wochentage)

Understanding days of the week is essential for making plans:

*   Montag (Monday) - *Ich gehe am **Montag** zur Arbeit.* (I go to work on **Monday**.)
*   Dienstag (Tuesday) - *Wir haben am **Dienstag** ein Meeting.* (We have a meeting on **Tuesday**.)
*   Mittwoch (Wednesday) - *Am **Mittwoch** gehe ich zum Sport.* (On **Wednesday**, I go to sports.)
*   Donnerstag (Thursday) - *Der **Donnerstag** ist fast Wochenende.* (**Thursday** is almost the weekend.)
*   Freitag (Friday) - *Ich freue mich auf den **Freitag**.* (I'm looking forward to **Friday**.)
*   Samstag (Saturday) - *Am **Samstag** schlafe ich lange.* (On **Saturday**, I sleep in.)
*   Sonntag (Sunday) - *Der **Sonntag** ist zum Entspannen da.* (**Sunday** is for relaxing.)

## Days of the Week
Here are the days of the week in German:

*   Montag (Monday)
*   Dienstag (Tuesday)
*   Mittwoch (Wednesday)
*   Donnerstag (Thursday)
*   Freitag (Friday)
*   Samstag (Saturday)
*   Sonntag (Sunday)

Here's a mnemonic to help you remember the weekdays:

<Mnemonic content="**M**y **D**ear **M**other **D**oes **F**ry" />

Here are some examples:

*   **Jeden Montag** habe ich Deutschkurs. (Every Monday I have a German course.)
*   Wir sehen uns am **nächsten Dienstag**. (We'll see each other next Tuesday.)
*   **Mittwochs** gehe ich immer einkaufen. (On Wednesdays, I always go shopping.)
*   Am **Donnerstagabend** gehen wir ins Kino. (On Thursday evening, we go to the cinema.)
*   Ich freue mich auf **Freitag**. (I'm looking forward to Friday.)
*   **Samstags** besuchen wir oft Freunde. (On Saturdays, we often visit friends.)
*   Der **Sonntag** ist mein Lieblingstag. (Sunday is my favorite day.)

<TipBox>
In German, the days of the week are always capitalized because they are nouns.
</TipBox>
