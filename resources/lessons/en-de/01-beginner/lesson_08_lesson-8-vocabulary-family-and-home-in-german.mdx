Welcome to Lesson 8! In this short introduction you'll learn common German words for family members and parts of the home. Read each German word aloud, notice the article (der/die/das), and try to say or write your own sentence.

Quick tip: German nouns are capitalized and usually come with an article showing gender. Practice saying the article + noun together.

Examples (say each aloud and repeat):
- **die Mutter** — mother
  Ich rufe die Mutter an. — I call the mother.
- **der Vater** — father
  Der Vater liest ein Buch. — The father is reading a book.
- **die Schwester** — sister
  Meine Schwester kocht in der Küche. — My sister cooks in the kitchen.
- **der Bruder** — brother
  Sein Bruder spielt im Garten. — His brother plays in the garden.
- **die Großmutter** — grandmother
  Die Großmutter wohnt im Haus. — The grandmother lives in the house.
- **der Großvater** — grandfather
  Der Großvater sitzt im Wohnzimmer. — The grandfather sits in the living room.
- **die Eltern** — parents
  Unsere Eltern sind zu Besuch. — Our parents are visiting.
- **das Kind** — child
  Ein Kind schläft im Schlafzimmer. — A child sleeps in the bedroom.
- **das Haus** — house
  Wir haben ein kleines Haus. — We have a small house.
- **die Wohnung** — apartment
  Ihre Wohnung hat ein großes Fenster. — Her apartment has a big window.
- **die Küche** — kitchen
  In der Küche gibt es einen Tisch. — There is a table in the kitchen.
- **das Badezimmer** — bathroom
  Das Badezimmer ist neben dem Schlafzimmer. — The bathroom is next to the bedroom.

Try this: choose three German words above and make your own short sentences. Say them out loud or write them down.

Understanding family and home in German-speaking countries helps you use vocabulary naturally and respectfully. Below are quick cultural pointers with many real examples to spot patterns and common usage.

## Family vocabulary — common terms
- Mutter — mother
- Vater — father
- Eltern — parents
- Schwester — sister
- Bruder — brother
- Oma (Großmutter) — grandma
- Opa (Großvater) — grandpa
- Tante — aunt
- Onkel — uncle
- Cousin / Cousine — cousin (m / f)
- Schwiegermutter — mother-in-law
- Schwager / Schwägerin — brother- / sister-in-law
- Enkel (m) / Enkelin (f) — grandson / granddaughter
- Ehemann / Ehefrau — husband / wife

## Home vocabulary — parts of the home
- Haus — house
- Wohnung — apartment
- Küche — kitchen
- Wohnzimmer — living room
- Schlafzimmer — bedroom
- Bad / Badezimmer — bathroom
- Garten — garden
- Balkon — balcony
- Flur — hallway
- Keller — basement
- Dachboden — attic
- Möbel — furniture (das Sofa, der Tisch, der Stuhl)

<TipBox>
- Use "Oma/Opa" and other diminutives in informal, affectionate contexts (common across families).
- With family you usually use "du" (informal); formal "Sie" is for non-family adults.
- Many German households use specific compound words (e.g., "Wohnzimmer" = living room). Learn compounds by recognizing parts (Wohn + Zimmer).
- When meeting someone’s family, polite phrases: "Schön, Sie kennenzulernen" (formal) or "Schön, dich kennenzulernen" (informal).
</TipBox>
