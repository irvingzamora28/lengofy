# Lesson 6: Personal Pronouns and Basic Sentence Structure

Welcome to Lesson 6. In this short introduction you'll meet German personal pronouns and practice building simple subject‑verb‑object sentences. Read the German examples aloud and repeat them for pronunciation practice.

| German pronoun | English | Pronunciation (guide) |
|---|---:|---|
| ich | I | ikh |
| du | you (sing.) | doo |
| er | he | air |
| sie | she / they / you (formal) | zee |
| es | it | es |
| wir | we | veer |
| ihr | you (pl.) | eer |
| Sie | you (formal) | zee (capital S) |

Practice sentences (say each one aloud; German first, then pronunciation, then English):
- Ich esse Brot. (ikh ESS-uh broht) — I eat bread.
- Du trinkst Wasser. (doo TRINKST VAH-sser) — You drink water.
- Er liest ein Buch. (air LEEST ayn bookh) — He reads a book.
- Sie schreibt eine Karte. (zee SHRYBT EYE-nuh KAR-tuh) — She writes a card.
- Es ist kalt. (es ist kahlt) — It is cold.
- Wir lernen Deutsch. (veer LER-nen DOYTCH) — We learn German.
- Ihr spielt Karten. (eer SHPEELT KAR-ten) — You (pl.) play cards.
- Sie arbeiten heute. (zee AR-bay-ten HOY-tuh) — They work today.
- Sie sind freundlich. (zee zint FROYND-likh) — You (formal) are friendly.
- Du hast ein Auto. (doo hahst ayn OW-toh) — You have a car.

Quick interactive task: choose any sentence above, change the subject to a different pronoun, and read the new sentence aloud three times. Focus on pronoun + verb agreement and clear pronunciation.

<TipBox>
- Basic sentence order: Subject + Verb + (Object). Example: Ich (S) esse (V) einen Apfel (O).
- Conjugate the verb to match the subject: ich bin, du bist, er ist, wir sind.
- Start with short sentences and swap subjects to practice different conjugations.
</TipBox>

Practice sentences to try aloud and translate (short list for drilling conjugation and word order):

- Ich esse einen Apfel. (I eat an apple.)

- Er geht nach Hause. (He goes home.)
- Sie hat ein Buch. (She has a book.)

- Sind Sie Herr Müller? (Are you Mr. Müller?)
- Ich heiße Anna. (My name is Anna.)

In simple German main clauses the typical order is Subject — Verb — Object (SVO). The subject (who) comes first, the conjugated verb (what they do) follows, then the direct object (what receives the action).

- Steps to build an SVO sentence:
  1. Identify the subject (who).
  2. Conjugate the verb for that subject.
  3. Place the object after the verb.

Common SVO examples (German — English):
- Ich sehe den Hund. — I see the dog.
- Du liest das Buch. — You read the book.
- Er isst einen Apfel. — He eats an apple.
- Sie schreibt einen Brief. — She writes a letter.
- Wir hören die Musik. — We hear the music.
- Ihr trinkt Wasser. — You (pl.) drink water.
- Sie kaufen ein Haus. — They buy a house.
- Ich kenne deinen Freund. — I know your friend.

</TipBox>
