## Introduction

Welcome to the lesson on greetings and introductions in German! In this section, we'll introduce the main topics we'll cover, which are essential for basic communication.

We will learn how to greet people formally and informally.

-   **Formal Greetings:** Used when addressing people you don't know well, elders, or in professional settings.

    -   Example: "Guten Tag" (Good day)
    -   Example: "Guten Morgen" (Good morning)
    -   Example: "Guten Abend" (Good evening)

-   **Informal Greetings:** Used with friends, family, and people you know well.

    -   Example: "Hallo" (Hello)
    -   Example: "Servus" (Hello/Goodbye - Primarily used in Southern Germany and Austria)
    -   Example: "Na?" (Hi - Short for "Na, wie geht's?" - How's it going?)

We will also explore how to introduce yourself and others.

-   **Introducing Yourself:**

    -   Example: "Ich bin [Your Name]." (I am [Your Name].)
    -   Example: "Mein Name ist [Your Name]." (My name is [Your Name].)

-   **Introducing Others:**

    -   Example: "Das ist [Person's Name]." (This is [Person's Name].)
    -   Example: "Darf ich Ihnen/dir [Person's Name] vorstellen?" (May I introduce you to [Person's Name]?) Formal/Informal

## Fundamentals of Greetings

Greetings in German vary depending on the context – whether it's formal or informal. Let's explore the fundamentals:

**Formal Greetings:**

Formal greetings are used when addressing people you don't know well, older individuals, or in professional settings.

-   **Guten Morgen** (Good morning): Used until about 11 AM.
-   **Guten Tag** (Good day/Good afternoon): Used from late morning until evening.
-   **Guten Abend** (Good evening): Used from evening until night.

**Informal Greetings:**

Informal greetings are suitable for friends, family, and people you know well.

-   **Hallo** (Hello): A general greeting, usable at any time of day.
-   **Moin** (Moin): Common in Northern Germany, similar to "Hello."
-   **Servus** (Servus): Common in Southern Germany and Austria, meaning "Hello" or "Goodbye."
-   **Grüß Gott** (Grüß Gott): Common in Southern Germany and Austria, meaning "Greet God."

-   Pay attention to regional variations.

## Travel-Specific Phrases

Here are some essential phrases to navigate social situations while traveling in German-speaking countries:

-   **"Entschuldigung, wo ist...?"** - Excuse me, where is...? (Use this to ask for directions.)
-   **"Wie viel kostet das?"** - How much does that cost? (Essential for shopping.)
-   **"Ich hätte gern..."** - I would like... (Useful for ordering food or drinks.)
-   **"Sprechen Sie Englisch?"** - Do you speak English? (Helpful if you're struggling with German.)
-   **"Ich verstehe das nicht."** - I don't understand that. (Polite way to indicate you need clarification.)
-   **"Könnten Sie das bitte wiederholen?"** - Could you please repeat that? (If you missed something.)
