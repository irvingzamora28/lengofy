## Introduction
¡Bienvenidos a la primera lección! Let's begin our journey into the Spanish alphabet and its pronunciation.

Spanish pronunciation is generally straightforward. Once you learn the basic sounds, you'll be able to pronounce most words correctly.

Here are some examples to get you started:

*   **Casa** (house): Pronounced as "Kah-sah."
*   **Perro** (dog): Pronounced as "peh-rroh."
*   **Gato** (cat): Pronounced as "Gah-toh."
*   **Sol** (sun): Pronounced as "Sol"
*   **Luna** (moon): Pronounced as "Loo-nah."
*   **Agua** (water): Pronounced as "Ah-gwah"
*   **Flor** (flower): Pronounced as "Flor"
*   **Arbol** (tree): Pronounced as "Ar-bol"

## Consonant Pronunciation
Let's explore the fascinating world of Spanish consonant pronunciation! While many Spanish consonants sound similar to their English counterparts, some key differences can significantly impact your pronunciation and comprehension.

*   **B and V:** These letters share the same pronunciation, a soft "b" sound. It's softer than the English "b," often approaching a "v" sound, but made with both lips.
