## Introduction

This lesson expands your vocabulary for talking about family and the home. You’ll also practice agreement and simple descriptive sentences to talk about your relatives and where you live.

<TipBox>
    Possessive adjectives (mi, tu, su, nuestro/a, sus, etc.) do not change for gender, but <strong>nuestro</strong> and <strong>nuestra</strong> do. All possessives change for number (mi/mis).
</TipBox>

## Agreement in Action

- Articles and adjectives must agree in gender and number with the noun.
- Pluralize nouns ending in a vowel with -s (ventana → ventanas) and consonant with -es (jardín → jardines).

| Correct (Agreement)       | Incorrect             | Why                               |
| ------------------------- | --------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Las casas pequeñas        | La casas pequeño      | Article/adjective must match (f/pl)|
| El dormitorio tranquilo   | La dormitorio tranquila| Gender mismatch                    |
| Los jardines bonitos      | Los jardín bonito     | Number mismatch                    |

## Quick Practice

Complete with the correct word and ensure agreement.

1) La ____ está cerrada. (puerta)

2) Mis ____ son simpáticos. (primo/prima → plural)

4) Nuestra ____ es grande. (casa)

5) Los ____ son tranquilos. (dormitorio → plural)


Answers:
