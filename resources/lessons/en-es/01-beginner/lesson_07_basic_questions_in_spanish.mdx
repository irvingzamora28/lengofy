## Introduction
¡Bienvenidos!

In this lesson, we'll explore essential questions for everyday conversations in Spanish. Mastering these questions will significantly boost your ability to interact and understand native speakers. Let's begin!

**Examples:**

*   **¿Cómo estás?** - How are you?
*   **¿Qué tal?** - What's up? / How's it going?
*   **¿Cómo te llamas?** - What's your name?
*   **¿De dónde eres?** - Where are you from?
*   **¿Cuántos años tienes?** - How old are you?
*   **¿A qué te dedicas?** - What do you do (for a living)?
*   **¿Qué hora es?** - What time is it?
*   **¿Entiendes?** - Do you understand?


## Question Words: Quién & Qué
Let's explore how to ask questions using **Quién** (who) and **Qué** (what).

**Quién** is used to ask about people. **Qué** is used to ask about things, actions or definitions.

### Quién (Who)

Use **Quién** to inquire about a person or people.

*   **¿Quién canta?** (Who is singing?)
    <TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson2/audio/quien_canta.mp3" />

    <TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson2/audio/quien_llama.mp3" />

*   **Dónde** always asks about a place.
*   **Cuándo** always asks about a time or date.

<TipBox>
When asking questions in Spanish, remember to use the inverted question mark (¿) at the beginning of the sentence!
</TipBox>


## Question Words: Por qué & Cómo
Let's learn how to ask questions using "Por qué" (why) and "Cómo" (how). These are essential for getting information and understanding situations.

### Por qué (Why)

"Por qué" is used to ask the reason *why* something is happening. It's always two words.

*   **¿Por qué estudias español?** (Why do you study Spanish?) <TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson6/audio/porque_estudias.mp3" />
*   **¿Por qué llegaste tarde?** (Why were you late?) <TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson6/audio/porque_llegaste.mp3" />
*   **¿Por qué estás triste?** (Why are you sad?) <TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson6/audio/porque_estas_triste.mp3" />
*   **¿Por qué no fuiste a la fiesta?** (Why didn't you go to the party?)
*   **¿Por qué compraste ese coche?** (Why did you buy that car?)
*   **¿Por qué cocinas tanto?** (Why do you cook so much?)

<TipBox>
"Porque" (one word) means "because" and is used to answer a "Por qué" question.
</TipBox>

### Cómo (How)

"Cómo" is used to ask *how* something is done, or to ask about someone's condition or state.

*   **¿Cómo estás?** (How are you?) <TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson6/audio/como_estas.mp3" miniPlayer={true} />
*   **¿Cómo te llamas?** (What's your name? - Literally, How do you call yourself?) <TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson6/audio/como_te_llamas.mp3" />
*   **¿Cómo se hace esto?** (How is this done?)
*   **¿Cómo llegaste aquí?** (How did you get here?)
*   **¿Cómo es tu casa?** (What is your house like?)
*   **¿Cómo cocinas el arroz?** (How do you cook rice?)
*   **¿Cómo puedo ayudarte?** (How can I help you?)

<Mnemonic content="Think of 'Cómo' as 'How' - they both start with 'Co'." />

Let's recap with a table:

| Question Word | Meaning  | Example Question                      |
| ------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Por qué       | Why      | ¿Por qué estudias español?            |
| Cómo          | How      | ¿Cómo estás?                          |
| Por qué       | Why      | ¿Por qué no comiste?                 |
| Cómo          | How      | ¿Cómo se prepara esta receta?         |
| Por qué       | Why      | ¿Por qué llegas tan temprano?        |
| Cómo          | How      | ¿Cómo funciona esta máquina?         |
| Por qué       | Why      | ¿Por qué siempre tienes razón?        |

<VoiceRecorder text="¿Cómo estás?" nativeAudio="/src/assets/courses/spanish/_shared/lessons/lesson6/audio/como_estas.mp3" />

Here are some steps to remember:

1.  Identify what you want to know: the reason (why) or the method/condition (how).
2.  Use "Por qué" for reasons and "Cómo" for methods or conditions.
3.  Make sure to include the question mark at the beginning and end of the question (¿ ?).
4.  Listen to examples and practice pronunciation.
