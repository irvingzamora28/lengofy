## Introduction

In Spanish, every noun has a grammatical gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural). Articles must agree with the noun they accompany. In this lesson, you will learn how to recognize noun gender and choose the correct definite and indefinite articles.

- "El libro" (the book) — masculine, singular.
- "La casa" (the house) — feminine, singular.
- "Los gatos" (the cats) — masculine, plural.
- "Unas flores" (some flowers) — feminine, plural.

<TipBox>
    Articles must match the noun in both gender and number: el/la (singular), los/las (plural) and un/una (singular), unos/unas (plural).
</TipBox>

## What is a Noun? Gender and Common Patterns

Nouns name people, places, things, or ideas. In Spanish, they are either masculine or feminine.

| Ending pattern | Typical gender | Examples |
| -------------- | -------------- | -------- |
| -o             | Masculine      | el lib**ro**, el cuadern**o**, el niñ**o** |
| -a             | Feminine       | la mes**a**, la cas**a**, la niñ**a** |
| -ción/-sión    | Feminine       | la canc**ión**, la profes**ión** |
| -dad/-tad      | Feminine       | la ciu**dad**, la liber**tad** |
| -ma (Greek origin) | Masculine  | el proble**ma**, el progra**ma** |

<TipBox>
    These are tendencies, not absolute rules. Always learn the article together with the noun (e.g., "el mapa", "la mano").
</TipBox>

## Definite Articles: “the” (el, la, los, las)

Use definite articles when you refer to specific nouns.

| Number     | Masculine | Feminine |
| ---------- | --------- | -------- |
| Singular   | el        | la       |
| Plural     | los       | las      |

Examples:

- El perro duerme. (The dog sleeps.)
- La escuela abre a las ocho. (The school opens at eight.)
- Los estudiantes estudian. (The students study.)
- Las llaves están aquí. (The keys are here.)

## Indefinite Articles: “a/an, some” (un, una, unos, unas)

Use indefinite articles when you refer to non-specific nouns.

| Number     | Masculine | Feminine |
| ---------- | --------- | -------- |
| Singular   | un        | una      |
| Plural     | unos      | unas     |

Examples:

- Quiero un café. (I want a coffee.)
- Necesito una silla. (I need a chair.)
- Compré unos zapatos. (I bought some shoes.)
- Hay unas manzanas en la mesa. (There are some apples on the table.)

<HighlightableText highlights='[{"word":"Una","info":"a (feminine singular)"},{"word":"flor","info":"flower"},{"word":"roja","info":"red (feminine singular)"}]'>

</HighlightableText>

## Special Case: Feminine Nouns with Stressed “a-” Using “el”

Some feminine nouns that begin with a stressed "a-" sound use "el" in the singular to ease pronunciation, but they remain feminine. In the plural, they use "las".

| Singular (article) | Noun      | Meaning   | Plural |
| ------------------ | --------- | --------- | ------ |
| el                 | agua      | water     | las aguas |
| el                 | águila    | eagle     | las águilas |
| el                 | hacha     | axe       | las hachas |

<HighlightableText highlights='[{"word":"El","info":"article used for pronunciation"},{"word":"agua","info":"water (feminine)"},{"word":"fría","info":"cold (feminine)"}]'>
El agua fría.
</HighlightableText>

<TipBox>
    Even with "el", adjectives still agree in feminine: "el águila **hermosa**", not "hermoso".
</TipBox>

## Plurals and Agreement

Form the plural of nouns and articles, and make adjectives agree in gender and number.

- Nouns ending in a vowel: add -s (libro → libros, casa → casas)
- Nouns ending in a consonant: add -es (papel → papeles, flor → flores)
- Z → C before -es (lápiz → lápices)

Examples with agreement:

- El coche rojo → Los coches rojos.
- La camisa blanca → Las camisas blancas.
- Un árbol alto → Unos árboles altos.

<HighlightableText highlights='[{"word":"Los","info":"the (masculine plural)"},{"word":"árboles","info":"trees"},{"word":"altos","info":"tall (masculine plural)"}]'>
Los árboles altos.
</HighlightableText>
