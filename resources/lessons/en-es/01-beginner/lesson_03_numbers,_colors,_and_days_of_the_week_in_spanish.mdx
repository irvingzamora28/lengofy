## Introduction
¡Bienvenidos! In this lesson, we'll embark on a colorful and numerical journey into the Spanish language. We'll cover essential vocabulary: numbers, colors, and the days of the week. This knowledge forms a crucial foundation for everyday communication in Spanish. Get ready to count, paint, and schedule your way to fluency!

Let's start with some examples of how this vocabulary will be useful:

*   **Numbers:** "Necesito *dos* manzanas." (I need *two* apples.)
*   **Colors:** "Mi carro es *azul*." (My car is *blue*.)
*   **Days of the Week:** "Voy al cine el *sábado*." (I'm going to the movies on *Saturday*.)

More examples:

*   **Numbers:** "Tengo *cinco* hermanos." (I have *five* siblings.)
*   **Colors:** "La casa es *blanca*." (The house is *white*.)
*   **Days of the Week:** "*Martes* es mi día favorito." (*Tuesday* is my favorite day.)

And even more:

*   **Numbers:** "Compré *tres* libros." (I bought *three* books.)
*   **Colors:** "El cielo es *gris* hoy." (The sky is *gray* today.)
*   **Days of the Week:** "Trabajo el *lunes*." (I work on *Monday*.)

Let's dive in!

## Days of the Week in Spanish
Let's learn the days of the week in Spanish! Understanding these will help you make plans and talk about time.

| Wednesday  | Miércoles  | MEE-air-koh-less            |
| Thursday   | Jueves     | HWAY-bess                   |
| Friday     | Viernes    | BEE-air-ness                  |

Here are some examples of how to use them in sentences:

*   Hoy es lunes. (Today is Monday.)
*   Mañana es martes. (Tomorrow is Tuesday.)
*   El miércoles voy al cine. (On Wednesday, I'm going to the movies.)
*   Trabajo los jueves y viernes. (I work on Thursdays and Fridays.)
*   El sábado voy a la fiesta. (On Saturday, I'm going to the party.)
*   El domingo descanso. (On Sunday, I rest.)
*   ¿Qué día es hoy? (What day is today?)
*   Nos vemos el lunes. (See you on Monday.)
*   Tengo una cita el martes. (I have an appointment on Tuesday.)
*   Voy a estudiar el miércoles. (I am going to study on Wednesday.)

<Mnemonic content="Lunes (LOO-ness) sounds like 'loony' - start the week with a loony Monday! " />

<TipBox>
Remember that the days of the week are generally masculine and are not capitalized in Spanish, except at the beginning of a sentence.
</TipBox>
