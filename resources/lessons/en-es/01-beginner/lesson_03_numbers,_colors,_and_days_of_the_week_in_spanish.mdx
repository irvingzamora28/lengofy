## Introduction

¡Bienvenidos! In this lesson, we'll embark on a colorful journey to learn essential Spanish vocabulary: numbers, colors, and days of the week. These are fundamental building blocks for communication and will help you express yourself in countless situations. Get ready to expand your Spanish horizons!

Let's start with some examples of why this vocabulary is so important:

-   "Tengo **dos** hermanos." (I have **two** brothers.)
-   "Mi carro es **rojo**." (My car is **red**.)
-   "Voy al cine el **sábado**." (I'm going to the movies on **Saturday**.)
-   "Necesito **tres** manzanas **verdes**." (I need **three green** apples.)
-   "El **lunes** es mi día favorito." (Monday is my favorite day.)
-   "Compré **cinco** camisas **azules**." (I bought **five blue** shirts.)
-   "El concierto es el **viernes**." (The concert is on **Friday**.)
-   "Quiero **una** camisa **amarilla**." (I want **one yellow** shirt.)
-   "Estudio español los **martes** y **jueves**." (I study Spanish on **Tuesdays** and **Thursdays**.)
-   "Tengo **diez** dedos." (I have **ten** fingers)

## Days of the Week in Spanish

Here's how to master the days of the week in Spanish and use them in daily conversations.

The days of the week in Spanish are:

| English   | Spanish   | Pronunciation   |
| --------- | --------- | --------------- |
| Monday    | Lunes     | LOO-ness        |
| Tuesday   | Martes    | MAR-tess        |
| Wednesday | Miércoles | MEE-air-ko-less |
| Thursday  | Jueves    | HWAY-bess       |
| Friday    | Viernes   | BEE-air-ness    |
| Saturday  | Sábado    | SAH-bah-doh     |
| Sunday    | Domingo   | doh-MEEN-goh    |

<TextToSpeechPlayer mp3File="../../assets/courses/en-es/_shared/lessons/lesson3/audio/days_of_week.mp3" />

Notice that the days of the week are not capitalized in Spanish unless they begin a sentence. Also, _lunes, martes, miércoles, jueves,_ and _viernes_ are masculine nouns. _Sábado_ and _domingo_ can be either masculine or feminine, but are most commonly used as masculine nouns.

Here are some examples of how to use the days of the week in Spanish:

-   **Hoy es lunes.** (Today is Monday.)
-   **Mañana es martes.** (Tomorrow is Tuesday.)
-   **El miércoles voy al cine.** (On Wednesday I'm going to the movies.)
-   **Los jueves tengo clases de español.** (On Thursdays I have Spanish classes.)
-   **El viernes salgo con mis amigos.** (On Friday I go out with my friends.)
-   **El sábado voy al mercado.** (On Saturday I go to the market.)
-   **El domingo descanso.** (On Sunday I rest.)
-   **Trabajo de lunes a viernes.** (I work from Monday to Friday.)
-   **¿Qué día es hoy?** (What day is today?)
-   **¿Qué día es mañana?** (What day is tomorrow?)
