## Introduction
¡Bienvenidos! This lesson will introduce you to personal pronouns and basic sentence structure in Spanish. Understanding these fundamentals is crucial for building more complex sentences and expressing yourself clearly. Let's dive in!

## Personal Pronouns: The Building Blocks

Personal pronouns are words that replace nouns, referring to specific people or things. Here are the most common personal pronouns in Spanish:

*   **Yo** - I (Example: **Yo** soy estudiante. - **I** am a student.)
*   **Tú** - You (informal, singular) (Example: **Tú** eres mi amigo. - **You** are my friend.)
*   **Él** - He (Example: **Él** es alto. - **He** is tall.)
*   **Ella** - She (Example: **Ella** es inteligente. - **She** is intelligent.)
*   **Usted** - You (formal, singular) (Example: **Usted** es el profesor. - **You** are the professor.)
*   **Nosotros/Nosotras** - We (masculine/feminine) (Example: **Nosotros** somos felices. - **We** are happy.  **Nosotras** somos estudiantes. - **We** are students (all female).)
*   **Vosotros/Vosotras** - You (informal, plural, used in Spain, but rarely in Latin America. We will not use it in this course.)
*   **Ellos/Ellas** - They (masculine/feminine) (Example: **Ellos** son mis padres. - **They** are my parents. **Ellas** son mis hermanas. - **They** are my sisters.)
*   **Ustedes** - You (formal or informal, plural, used in Latin America) (Example: **Ustedes** son bienvenidos. - **You** are welcome.)

## Basic Sentence Structure: Subject + Verb (+ Complement)

The most basic sentence structure in Spanish is:

**Subject + Verb (+ Complement)**

*   **Subject:** The person or thing performing the action (often a personal pronoun).
*   **Verb:** The action word.
*   **Complement:** Additional information (optional).

Let's look at some examples:

*   **Yo hablo español.** (I speak Spanish.)
    *   Yo (Subject) + hablo (Verb) + español (Complement)
*   **Ella canta bien.** (She sings well.)
    *   Ella (Subject) + canta (Verb) + bien (Complement)
*   **Nosotros comemos pizza.** (We eat pizza.)
    *   Nosotros (Subject) + comemos (Verb) + pizza (Complement)
*   **Tú estudias mucho.** (You study a lot.)
    *   Tú (Subject) + estudias (Verb) + mucho (Complement)
*   **Él trabaja aquí.** (He works here.)
    *   Él (Subject) + trabaja (Verb) + aquí (Complement)
*   **Ustedes aprenden rápido.** (You learn quickly.)
    *   Ustedes (Subject) + aprenden (Verb) + rápido (Complement)
*   **Ellas bailan salsa.** (They dance salsa.)
    *   Ellas (Subject) + bailan (Verb) + salsa (Complement)
*   **Usted lee el libro.** (You read the book.)
    *   Usted (Subject) + lee (Verb) + el libro (Complement)

## Verb Conjugation: The Verb 'Ser'
The verb "ser" is one of the most important verbs in Spanish. It means "to be" and is used to describe permanent or lasting attributes, such as origin, characteristics, or identity. Here’s how to conjugate it in the present tense:

| Pronoun        | Conjugation | Example Sentence                | Translation                 |
| -------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Yo             | soy         | Yo soy estudiante.              | I am a student.             |
| Tú             | eres        | Tú eres mi amigo.                | You are my friend.          |
| Él / Ella / Ud.| es          | Él es alto. / Ella es doctora.   | He is tall. / She is a doctor. |
| Nosotros/as    | somos       | Nosotros somos felices.           | We are happy.               |
| Vosotros/as    | sois        | Vosotros sois de España.        | You are from Spain.         |
| Ellos / Ellas / Uds. | son         | Ellos son inteligentes.          | They are intelligent.       |
