## Introduction

In this lesson, you’ll learn the core personal pronouns in Spanish and how to build simple sentences using a clear Subject–Verb–Object (SVO) pattern. You’ll also see when and why Spanish often drops the subject pronoun.

<TipBox>
    Spanish is a pro-drop language: the subject pronoun is often omitted because the verb ending already indicates who the subject is.
</TipBox>

## Personal Pronouns

| Person | Singular     | Plural               | Notes |
| ------ | ------------- | -------------------- | ----- |
| 1st    | yo            | nosotros / nosotras  | nosotras for all-female groups |
| 2nd    | tú / usted    | ustedes              | usted (formal singular); ustedes is plural in Latin America |
| 3rd    | él / ella     | ellos / ellas        | Gendered third-person forms |

<TipBox>
    In Latin America, use <strong>ustedes</strong> for plural “you.” In Spain, <strong>vosotros/vosotras</strong> is common in informal contexts.
</TipBox>

## Basic Sentence Structure (SVO)

The most neutral Spanish sentence order is Subject–Verb–Object (SVO). Word order is flexible, but SVO is a great starting point.

| Pattern | Example (Spanish) | Translation |
| ------- | ----------------- | ----------- |
| SVO     | Yo leo libros.    | I read books. |
| SVO     | Ella come pan.    | She eats bread. |
| SVO     | Ellos ven la casa.| They see the house. |

Sometimes the subject is dropped when it’s clear from the verb ending:

- Leo libros. (I read books.)
- Comemos pan. (We eat bread.)

## Agreement Essentials

- Verbs must agree with the subject in person and number.
- Articles and adjectives must agree with nouns in gender and number.

| Agreement | Correct | Incorrect | Why |
| --------- | ------- | --------- | --- |
| Subject–Verb | Nosotros vivimos | Nosotros vive | Verb must match 1st person plural |
| Noun–Adj. | Las casas grandes | Las casa grande | Plural feminine agreement required |

## Dropping the Subject Pronoun

It’s common and natural to omit the subject pronoun when context makes it clear.

| With Pronoun | Without Pronoun | Translation |
| ------------ | ---------------- | ----------- |
| Yo estudio.  | Estudio.         | I study. |
| Ella vive aquí. | Vive aquí.     | She lives here. |
| Nosotros comemos. | Comemos.    | We eat. |

## Quick Practice

Fill in the blank with the correct pronoun or reorder to SVO.

1) ___ leo el periódico. (I)

2) Somos de Perú. (Identify the pronoun: ___)

3) ___ vive en Madrid. (she)

4) Reorder to SVO: "come Juan pan" → ___

5) Complete: "___ comemos arroz" (we)


Answers:

- Yo leo el periódico.
- Nosotros / Nosotras (depending on the group)
- Ella vive en Madrid.
- Juan come pan.
- Nosotros/Nosotras comemos arroz.
